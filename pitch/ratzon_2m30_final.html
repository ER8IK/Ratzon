Speaker notes - 30 sec

With Ratzon, the user can write: "Swap 50 USDT TRC20 to BTC." Ratzon understands the intent, detects the source network, prepares the route, shows the minimum amount, and asks for the BTC payout address. Before payment details are issued, Ratzon checks that the address matches the expected network. If the address is wrong, the flow is blocked before funds move.

Speaker notes - 25 sec

Under the hood, Ratzon uses a fast regex parser and QVAC for more complex or voice-based intents. The intent goes to a dispatcher, then to a protocol router. Jupiter handles live Solana swaps, while guarded adapters handle Smart Swap, Drift, Kamino, and staking-style flows. Then the risk and address safety layers make the next action clear.
